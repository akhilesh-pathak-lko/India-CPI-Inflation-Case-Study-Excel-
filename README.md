# India-CPI-Inflation-Case-Study-Excel-
This project analyzes India’s Consumer Price Index (CPI) to understand inflation trends across different categories like food, housing, transport, and healthcare. It focuses on identifying key drivers of inflation and external impacts such as COVID-19 and oil price fluctuations.

## Key Insights

- Food category is the highest contributor to overall CPI inflation, showing consistent impact across months.

- Inflation showed a significant spike post-2020, largely driven by COVID-19 disruptions in supply chains and demand patterns.

- The highest YoY inflation was observed during the pandemic/recovery phase due to economic instability and price surges.

- Food inflation remained highly volatile, with sharp month-on-month fluctuations driven by seasonal and supply-side factors.

- Essential categories like healthcare and food saw noticeable inflation increases during COVID-19.

- Transportation and fuel-related categories showed strong correlation with crude oil price fluctuations.

- Oil price increases (2021–2023) had a direct impact on inflation, especially in energy and logistics-related sectors.

- Inflation trends indicate that external global factors and domestic supply shocks both play a major role.

- Overall CPI inflation is driven by a combination of food prices, fuel costs, and macroeconomic events.

- Data analysis highlights the importance of monitoring category-level trends rather than only overall CPI.
